## 1. Family Law (Legal)

**Audience:** Family law attorneys, judges, GALs
**Voice:** Expert consultant. Authoritative, direct, confident.
**Positioning:** "We know what you don't — and that's what's costing you cases."

**Tone keywords:** Sharp, knowledgeable, peer-level, no fluff
**Perspective:** First-person from Michael as industry expert speaking to fellow professionals

**Key messages:**
- What you don't know about drug testing hurts your case
- We fill the knowledge gap — before, during, and after the test
- We help you pick the right test, not just run one
- Chain-of-custody and confirmation testing matter — a doctor's office test won't survive cross-examination
- Hair testing (90 days head / up to 1 year body hair), urine EtG (up to 80 hours), PEth (3–4 weeks)

**Do:**
- Speak as an equal to legal professionals
- Use specific technical details (panels, detection windows, confirmation testing)
- Frame everything around case outcomes — winning and losing
- Challenge assumptions ("that test your client's doctor ran?")

**Don't:**
- Talk down or over-explain legal concepts they already know
- Use soft or emotional language — these are professionals making strategic decisions
- Be salesy or use discount/promo language
- Use jargon without making it relevant to their case

## 2. DOT / Transportation

**Audience:** Safety directors, fleet managers, owner-operators (trucking industry)
**Voice:** Straight-talking partner. Clear, no-BS, blue collar friendly.
**Positioning:** "Simple pricing. Nationwide coverage. You stay compliant. That's it."

**Tone keywords:** Plain-spoken, reliable, practical, zero fluff
**Perspective:** First-person from Michael or TrueTest as a company — "we" voice

**Key messages:**
- Straightforward pricing — no hidden fees, no surprise invoices
- Nationwide coverage so your drivers can test anywhere
- When you call, you get a real person who actually knows DOT compliance
- We keep you compliant so you keep rolling — that's the whole point
- Personal service, not a 1-800 number and a hold queue

**Do:**
- Keep it simple — short sentences, plain words
- Lead with price and convenience
- Speak their language — these are people running businesses, not reading whitepapers
- Emphasize the human element (real people answering real questions)

**Don't:**
- Use corporate buzzwords or marketing fluff
- Overcomplicate compliance — make it feel manageable
- Be condescending about regulations — they know the stakes
- Bury the price or dance around cost

## 3. Parental DNA Testing

**Audience:** Individuals — often blue collar, price-sensitive, emotionally stressed
**Voice:** Reassuring and straightforward. Calm, clear, no judgment.
**Positioning:** "Accurate results. Affordable price. Confidential. Period."

**Tone keywords:** Warm but direct, accessible, no judgment, affordable
**Perspective:** TrueTest as a company — "we" voice, welcoming

**Key messages:**
- Affordable DNA testing — don't overpay for answers you need
- Court-admissible results through CLIA-certified and federally accredited labs
- Confidential — your results stay yours
- Walk-ins welcome, no appointment needed
- At-home kits available for informational (non-legal) purposes

**Do:**
- Lead with price and simplicity
- Use plain, accessible language — no medical jargon
- Acknowledge that this is a big deal without being dramatic about it
- Make the process feel easy and non-intimidating
- Be clear about the difference between legal and informational testing

**Don't:**
- Use clinical or cold language
- Make assumptions about why someone needs a test
- Be preachy or moralistic
- Use language that feels like upselling

## 4. Mobile / Onsite Testing

**Audience:** HR departments, companies with 3rd shift workers, large-volume employers, attorneys with difficult clients
**Voice:** Problem-solver. Efficient, capable, removes excuses.
**Positioning:** "We come to you. No excuses. No delays. Same forensic-grade testing."

**Tone keywords:** Capable, efficient, flexible, solution-oriented
**Perspective:** TrueTest as a company — "we" voice

**Key messages:**
- We come to your location — office, warehouse, job site
- Same certified technicians, same chain-of-custody, same defensible results
- Perfect for 3rd shift, large groups, or clients who "can't make it"
- We remove every excuse for not getting tested
- For attorneys: wealthy client won't go to a collection site? We go to them. Client says they can't make it? We eliminate that excuse.

**Do:**
- Emphasize convenience without sacrificing credibility
- Tailor the pitch to the audience — HR cares about logistics, attorneys care about eliminating excuses
- Highlight that mobile doesn't mean lesser quality
- Be direct about the problem this solves

**Don't:**
- Make it sound like a lesser option than in-lab testing
- Ignore the cross-sell angle — mobile is relevant to all four business lines
- Overcomplicate scheduling or logistics in the messaging
